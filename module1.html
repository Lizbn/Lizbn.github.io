CUIN 7356 Mini-research proposal

Why is the study important?

Webcasts are ubiquitous in secondary education. This study demonstrates the comparative effectiveness of webcast instruction with didactic instruction.

Who are the subjects/participants of the proposed study?

The participants are two groups of students selected from the STEM Academy student body at Hogg Middle School, HISD

What is the basic research question?

In 7th and 8th grade HISD students, is autonomous viewing of a webcast as effective as traditional teaching methods for a single STEM module.

Where would the study be conducted?

In the after school STEM Academy meetings at Hogg Middle School or Lamar High School.

What is the time frame for the study?

The time frame would be coordinated to coincide with in-class instruction of recorded module during the first or second week of February. The instruction and initial data collection will take place during the Tuesday meeting and semi-structured interviews will take place during the Thursday meeting of the same week.

What type of data would be collected?

All participants will answer a 10 question assessment immediately after instruction as part of their regular class time. Semi-structured interviews will be recorded.

How might the data be analyzed?

The assessments will be graded and basic statistical analysis will be used to compare experimental and control groups. The interview transcripts will be coded and categorized.
